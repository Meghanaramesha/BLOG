The story of how that happened starts with a guy who wanted to quit The Spark That Didn’t Want to Be a Fire In April 2013, most of Hanoi was in the streets celebrating Reunification Day, the annual holiday marking the end of the American War. But in a bedroom in Van Phuc, a silk-making village on the outskirts of the city, a 28-year-old programmer sat alone with his iPhone and a poster of Mario. Dong Nguyen had a day job programming GPS devices for taxis. He made games on the side because he missed the simplicity of the Nintendo games he’d grown up playing on cloned consoles his family could barely afford.
