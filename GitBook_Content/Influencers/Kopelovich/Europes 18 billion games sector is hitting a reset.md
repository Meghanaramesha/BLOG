Europe’s $18 billion games sector is hitting a reset. And it says a lot about where the global industry is heading next. After a decade of consolidation-heavy growth, Europe’s publishers are now facing a harder truth: the era of easy capital is over. What matters now is operating discipline, focused creativity, and the ability to adapt to tightening platform economics.Three firms on three different paths tell the story:•CD PROJEKT RED is the recovery case. After Cyberpunk’s collapse, it rebuilt quickly and decisively: cut costs, reorganized teams, and regained fan trust.•Ubisoftis the restructuring case. Postponed earnings, layoffs, reliance on older titles. Fighting to stay independent while investors and players grow restless.•Modern Times Group MTG ABis the reinvention case.
