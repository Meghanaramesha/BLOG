I analyze the first 1–2 hours of player experience. Not the whole game, just the entrance. How players learn the rules, where they pause, and where they quietly drop out.After reviewing many large-scale RPGs, MMOs, and ARPGs, I kept noticing a pattern. Not a failure reason or a theory, just something that appears consistently in early gameplay. A kind of obscurity.A world that doesn’t read clearly at the start.So this isn’t a post about “why games fail.”It’s simply a UX question:Is this early obscurity actually workable for new players? ⸻1.
