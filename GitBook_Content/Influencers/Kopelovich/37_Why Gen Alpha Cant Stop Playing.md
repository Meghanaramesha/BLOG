### [Why Gen Alpha Can’t Stop Playing](https://www.gamigion.com/why-gen-alpha-cant-stop-playing/)

How the evolution of game design turned playtime into an economy of attention. Photo by Jordan Whitt on Unsplash When I played games in the 1990s, things were simple. You bought a game, or more often, got a pirated copy from a friend, installed it, and played until you finished it, or you got a new one to play. Games were offline and finite. You could play to the end, see the credits roll, and that was it. There were no updates, no notifications, no constant live events. Games were also premium products. You paid for them once, and the transaction ended there. The business model rewarded making great experiences that people wanted to buy. There were also more games; at least the perception for me was that you’d cycle through a lot more games in a year. Today, everything is the opposite. […]
