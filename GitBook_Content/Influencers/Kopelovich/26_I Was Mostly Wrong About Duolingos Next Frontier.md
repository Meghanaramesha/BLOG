### [I Was Mostly Wrong About Duolingo’s Next Frontier](https://www.gamigion.com/i-was-mostly-wrong-about-duolingos-next-frontier/)

Duolingo has reshaped how millions learn languages. Its playful lessons and streak mechanics have become the template for consumer learning apps. I believed the company’s next frontier would be opening the platform to user-generated content (UGC). Learners want more: niche dialects, cultural modules, business vocab, local idioms - things Duolingo can’t build at scale. That logic still makes sense. But I was only half right. Where I was correct is this: Duolingo can only move as fast as its internal teams. That has always been the bottleneck. But where I was wrong, is in assuming UGC was the obvious solution. Duolingo is pursuing a different strategy, and the way they got there mirrors a powerful lesson from the company’s CEO.
